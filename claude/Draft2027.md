# Alps 2027 — Draft Tour Plan

**Munich → Dolomites → Stelvio → Livigno → Bernina → Splügen → [FORK] → Munich**

Status: draft, June 2027 (dates TBD). Tandem with Boen (12) as stoker. **No support vehicle** — everything carried on the bikes, all transfers by train. Bowen likely not on this trip (high school).

**30 days either way.** The route forks at Splügen (Day 20/21). Both branches are live.

---

## Design principles (carried from 2026)

1. **Fixed bases, optional rides.** The 2026 Hospental model: lock lodging for multiple nights, let weather decide which rides happen. Oberalp and Gotthard were bonuses, not schedule. No floating "weather spare days" — resilience lives in the structure.
2. **Train the segments with no cycling value.** Ride what's worth riding.
3. **Paved corridors for the loaded tandem.** Cross-check Google Maps paved routing against RWGPS/Komoot, which snap to Veloland/unpaved connectors (see 2026 Col des Mosses).
4. **No support vehicle.** Self-supported; all transfers by train. Multi-night bases mean luggage sits still on rest days and bonus rides.
5. **Captain power 100–125W NP on big cols.** Proven range across the entire 2026 tour.

### Lindau logistics (finishes A and B)
- Bayern-Ticket + per-bike day tickets. **No reservations.**
- **First direct RE 07:45** (next 09:45). **Stay the night in Lindau** — it's an *origin* station, racks start empty. This is what has always been done.
- Lustenau is the last cheap stop before the lake (16.45 mi / 1h30m out, flat) — fallback if Lindau is booked, but means a 05:45 rollout.
- **Bregenz is oddly pricier than Lindau.** Not a candidate.
- **Allgäu exit rejected** — no direct Oberstdorf–Munich train (all change at Memmingen).

## Open items

### Snow / opening dates
- **Stelvio (2,758m)** — **the sole binding constraint.** Sets the earliest start. Everything else is 2,000–2,478m and low-risk by mid-June.
- **Nufenen (2,478m)** *(South)* — second-highest on the route. Worth checking, though it typically opens by late May/early June.
- **Forcola di Livigno (2,315m)** — **not a constraint.** If closed: Munt la Schera tunnel bike shuttle → Ova Spin → RhB Zernez → Pontresina. Easy, just expensive. Note it skips Bernina's south side — a real loss, not a free swap.

### ⚠ Train line closures — check winter 2026/27

**Three of the four Italian/Austrian segments run on lines under active works.** The 2025 Bolzano–Merano surprise forced an unplanned century; the 2026 Ivrea–Aosta closure went fine *because it was known early*.

**The universal gotcha: bikes are not allowed on rail replacement buses.** When a line goes down, the bus won't take you — you ride.

| Segment | Status | If down |
|---|---|---|
| **Pustertal** (Bruneck→Toblach) | Closed **30 Mar – 28 Apr 2026** for **ETCS signalling** + maintenance, Bruneck–Innichen. Routine rollout, deliberately in the spring shoulder window | **Costs a full day.** ⚠ *Not* a Riggertal issue — Riggertal is at the western end (Brixen/Vahrn/Natz-Schabs) and doesn't touch this segment |
| **Brenner** (Innsbruck→summit) | ÖBB shut Innsbruck–Bolzano **18 Jul–1 Aug in both 2025 and 2026** — a bundled annual works window. **Riggertal tie-in still unfinished** (Natz-Schabs station still closed Jul 2026), so expect a 2027 repeat | **Costs a full day** — Innsbruck (574m) → Brenner (1,370m) is ~800m, and the Eisacktal descent still has to happen after |
| **Vinschgau** (Merano→Prad) | Fully closed Oct 2025–Mar 2026 for electrification; resumed 29 Mar 2026, initially reduced | **A hard day, not a day**: Merano→Trafoi ≈ 65km / 1,200m. Check whether the **BikeLiner bike shuttle** (Merano→Mals) still runs — it exists because the buses won't carry bikes |
| **Holzkirchen** (Munich S-Bahn) | No known works | Dullness + the flat-inducing Isar path |

**The one question, winter 2026/27:** *is there an ETCS/maintenance closure on Bruneck–Innichen in 2027, and is it in the spring shoulder window or the summer?* Both 2026 South Tyrol closures were placed outside tourist season. If that holds, June is clear.

## Notes on the hard days

Only **two** stages qualify as genuinely big:

- **Day 20 — Chiavenna → Splügen:** ~1,780m. Single climb.

### Train segments — the pattern
Trains are used for exactly the stretches with no cycling value:
- **S-Bahn → Holzkirchen** (flat-inducing Isar path)
- **Innsbruck → Brenner summit** (motorway corridor)
- **Bruneck → Toblach** (dull Pustertal drag)
- **Merano → Spondinig/Prad** (boring Vinschgau false-flat)
- *North:* Thusis → Tiefencastel (busy Schinstrasse) · Innsbruck → Scharnitz (Zirler Berg, finish C)
- *South:* **Bellinzona → Airolo** (Ticino heat)
- **The return:** Lindau → Munich (North, walk-on RE) or **Zurich → Munich** (South, EC, reservation required)
